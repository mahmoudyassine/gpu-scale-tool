Hardware & resilience
Bandwidth sets decode speed, dense FP16 Tensor-Core TFLOPS sets prefill speed, VRAM sets what fits. 2026 parts (VR200, MI455X) carry pre-launch estimates.
What the fleet must keep serving through. Advanced mode offers the full set of patterns (N+2, mirrors, active/active splits) with their economics.resilience
Pick what the deployment must survive. The sizing rule: every figure in the results is what you can guarantee even during the covered failure, so throughput and concurrency never grow with resilience. The extra hardware either idles until a failure (spares, mirrors, DR standby) or serves day-to-day as burst headroom (Active/Active). The economics strip under the topology shows guaranteed vs normal-day capacity, idle hardware and the cost multiplier for the chosen model.
%
How full Auto-size may pack the serving GPUs. 80% leaves industry-standard growth headroom; 90 to 95% packs tighter at the cost of burst and growth margin (the thin-headroom warning starts at 92%). Manual sliders are never limited by this.
